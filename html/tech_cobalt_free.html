Driven by relentless innovation in materials, Cobalt-free

Cobalt is one of key battery materials. Due to its high price, LFP batteries have come on the market as an alternative. However, this is not the case for Samsung SDI. Instead, Samsung SDI decided to develop new Co-free battery that is more affordable but has higher energy density than LFP battery.

Cathode materials, one of materials that constitute secondary batteries, determine the battery capacity and output and also account for 40% of battery production costs. Anode materials are usually made up of nickel, manganese, cobalt, aluminum and others in a proper ratio. Among them, cobalt plays a critical role in enhancing battery safety and life cycle.

The problem is the high price of cobalt. So, EV makers including Tesla, Volkswagen, and Daimler, opted for LFP batteries for their affordable electric vehicles given price competitiveness. However, despite high stability, fast charging and affordable prices, LFP batteries have limited output due to slow diffusion of lithium ions and low ion conductivity.

Meanwhile, end-customers are increasingly asking for affordable prices of EVs in line with commercialization of EVs. Accordingly, Samsung SDI has diversified the market segment into premium and mass market and developed Co-free batteries to realize affordable prices.

Co-free batteries are expected to play a key role in affordable EV market in the future, based on higher energy density and price competitiveness than LFP batteries. With know-hows in materials technology and successful experiences in mass-production, Samsung SDI aims to complete the development of Co-free batteries by 2025.
