Absolute Safety, Direct Injection System for ESS

Direct injection system, presented by Samsung SDI at ESS Battery Tech Day in 2021, is an automatic fire suppression system using heat-sensing tubes. In case of fires, the system detects an event immediately and injects fire agent directly to the event cell. Let’s learn about Samsung SDI’s reliable and proven fire suppression using direction injection system.

In 2019, Samsung SDI announced a drastic safety measure to install a fire extinguishing system in its old or new battery modules after a series of fires were reported in ESS equipped with its batteries. Since then, Samsung SDI has spent more than 100 billion won for six months to retrieve the supplied ESS and install the fire extinguishing system.

The fire extinguishing system installed at the upper side of ESS modules suppresses fires by automatically releasing fire agent contained in capsules if abnormal temperature is detected.

 The system was capable enough of controlling fire but Samsung SDI found there was a need to advance the existing capsule-type system to preemptively suppress fire caused by heat propagation as batteries are likely to generate much heat due to continued capacity increase.

In this regard, Samsung SDI has developed direct injection system, a proprietary ESS safety technology as a new way to suppress fires in ESS. Direct injection system using heat-sensing tubes injects fire agent directly to the event cell after detecting heat.

Spray pipelines are installed across racks and fire agent in the pipelines will be automatically released and injected to the event cell once heat is detected. Vents and heat-sensing tubes will be installed in parallel so each tube can target a cell in case of fires effectively.

Samsung SDI’s direct injection system has passed the UL9540A test, the U.S. safety certification. Direct injection system prevents issues that can trigger fires in ESS in advance so that ESS can be operated and managed in a safer manner.
